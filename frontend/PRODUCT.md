# SFA — Stats Football Award

## Product Purpose
A premium football statistics platform that measures each player's real impact through a proprietary scoring system (SFA Points) that accounts for opponent level, competition, game moment, and difficulty. The design serves the data — not the other way around.

## Register
product

## Users
- Football fans who follow multiple leagues and want analytical depth beyond basic stats
- Fantasy football players making data-driven decisions
- Analysts and enthusiasts who value statistical context over raw numbers

## Brand
Dark, editorial, premium. Inspired by ESPN and FIFA but more personal and severe. The aesthetic is precision-driven, not entertainment-driven. Data has editorial weight.

**Voice:** Precise, confident, brief. State facts; let the numbers speak.
- Correct: "Lamine Yamal leads with 10.146 SFA points."
- Wrong: "Yamal is ON FIRE! The best in La Liga!"

**Visual identity:** DARK-ONLY. There is no light mode and there will never be one. The product lives in darkness because football data at night, on a screen, in concentration — that is the context.

## Design Constraints
- Background: `#111111` (primary), `#0a0a0a` (navbar/deep)
- Accent: `#C9A84C` gold — used ONLY for SFA points, rankings, and positions. Never decorative.
- Text: `#ffffff` primary, `#aaaaaa` secondary, `#555555` faint/labels
- Typography: Barlow Condensed UPPERCASE 700–800 (display), Inter 400 (body), Space Mono (numbers/dates)
- Border-radius: max 4–6px. No pill shapes, no large roundings.
- No emojis anywhere in the UI.

## Anti-References
- ESPN, SofaScore, Transfermarkt: too bright, too busy, too generic
- Generic dark SaaS dashboards with purple/teal gradients
- Neon glow effects common in "gaming" or "crypto" aesthetics
- Light-mode sports designs
- Card-heavy layouts with equal-sized boxes repeated in 3-column grids

## Strategic Principles
- Gold (`#C9A84C`) is earned — it signals SFA score and rank only. Any other use dilutes its meaning.
- Section titles follow a strict pattern: Barlow Condensed UPPERCASE 11–12px, `letter-spacing: 0.15em`, `color: #aaaaaa`, with `border-left: 2px solid #C9A84C; padding-left: 8px`.
- Data rows breathe without cards unless elevation is functionally needed.
- The Ranking page top-3 showcase (hero cards) is the one place for large player photos and bold SFA numbers. Everything else is compact table data.
- Player profile pages follow a clear hierarchy: header → quick stats → action breakdown → fixture history.
